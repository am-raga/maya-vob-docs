Navigate the dashboard

Your home base for benefits verification. Here’s what each region on the screen does.

2 min read
Version 2.3 · June 2026
Maya VOB runs automatically — most patients appear here without any action from you. The dashboard is for review, not initiation. To add a patient manually, see H2: Start a benefits inquiry.

Dashboard at a glance

The dashboard has five regions. The top bar holds product navigation and your profile — it stays fixed as you scroll.

Five regions
1
Header filters — Doctor and Date Range. Scope everything on the page.
2
Summary cards — Total VOBs, Generated, Not Generated.
3
Operations panel — Add patient and Upload patients for manual flows.
4
Status filters — chips to narrow the table by case state.
5
Patient table — the working list. Click any row to open a patient.
1

Header filters

The Doctor and Date Range filters scope everything on the page. Date Range filters by service date (appointment date, not when the VOB was generated). Click Reset to clear both.

2

Summary cards

Three counts reflecting your current filter selection.

What each card counts
1
Total VOBs — all patient cases in the system for your current filters.
2
Generated VOBs — cases where benefits are verified and the document is ready.
3
Not Generated VOBs — cases the agent couldn’t complete. See R3 for common reasons.

Click any summary card to filter the patient table to only show cases in that state.

3

Operations panel

Two quick-action cards on the right for manual flows — walk-ins, same-day adds, and urgent cases not covered by the automated run.

  • Add patient (Start Now) — single patient entry. See H2a.
  • Upload patients (Batch Processing) — CSV bulk upload. See H2b.
4

Status filters

Click a chip to narrow the patient table to that state. Click again to clear. Full definitions in R1: Status states.

StatusWhat it means
In DraftBenefits verified, awaiting your review and confirmation. Editable.
Cost FinalizedCost estimate confirmed and locked. Requires a Re-VOB to change.
Pending ActionFlagged for staff input — typically a missing or ambiguous data point.
Inactive / Not VerifiedInsurance inactive or could not be verified.
5

Patient table

The working list. Click any row to open the patient’s cost details page.

Toolbar

  • Search Patient — search by name within the current filtered list.
  • Service Date sort — toggle ascending/descending by appointment date.
  • Filter — filter by insurance carrier.
  • Show PII — toggle to mask patient names for screen sharing.

Columns

  • Patient Name — last, first. Avatar shows initials.
  • Service Date — patient’s upcoming or past appointment date.
  • Benefits Verified On — when the VOB was last generated. — if not yet run.
  • Provider — treating provider for this visit.
  • Insurance Provider — payer as captured from the EHR.
  • Status — current state badge. See Section 4 above.

Manually start a benefits inquiry for a walk-in, same-day add, or urgent case.

2 min read
Version 2.3 · June 2026
Most patients appear automatically from your EHR schedule. Use this flow only when a patient isn’t already on the dashboard.
1

Click Add patient

From the dashboard, click Add patient in the Operations panel (top right). The Add Patient modal opens.

2

Fill in required fields

All fields marked with an asterisk are required:

FieldNotes
First name & Last nameAs shown on insurance card.
Date of BirthFormat: MMDDYYYY
Insurance ProviderDropdown-driven. Select from list to ensure correct payer routing.
Policy NumberMember ID from insurance card.
Service DateFormat: MMDDYYYY — the appointment date.

  • Provider name — treating provider for this visit.

The benefits document completes in less than 20 seconds. The cost calculator takes approximately 1 minute to unlock after the document is ready. The page auto-refreshes and updates status automatically.

Access and print a VOB document

Open the benefits inquiry document generated by RagaAI for any patient, review coverage, and print or download.

2 min read
Version 2.3 · June 2026
1

Click a patient row

From the dashboard, click anywhere on a patient row to open their profile.

2

Open the Benefits Document

The Verification Hub loads. Click Open Document under the Benefits Document card.

3

Review the document

The benefits document is a scrollable payer summary. It includes:

  • Plan Information (carrier, plan type, in-network status, member ID, effective dates)
  • Deductible and Out-of-Pocket amounts (individual and family)
  • Authorizations (Prior Auth, Referral, Pre-existing limitations)
  • Service Benefits by CPT group (Allergy Testing, Immunotherapy, Rapid Desensitization, Respiratory)
  • Comments from payer response

Verify the details match your expectations. If something looks wrong, see H5: Handle a low-confidence VOB or H6: Edit a VOB.

4

Download or print

Click Download in the top toolbar. Your browser downloads the document as a PDF. Open it locally to print.

Retrying a VOB

Re-run benefits verification to get a fresh payer response.

2 min read
Version 2.3 · June 2026
What Re-VOB does
A Re-VOB always fetches the most current information directly from the payer. Unlike payer call notes, it reflects real-time eligibility data.

When to use

  • The deductible or OOP balance may have changed because claims were processed after the last VOB was generated.
  • The VOB failed on the first attempt — visible in the Comments section of the benefits document. See R3: Why a VOB may fail.
1

Open the patient record

Click the patient row on the dashboard to open their Verification Hub.

2

Click Retry VOB

A confirmation modal appears. It warns that re-running will overwrite any manual changes you’ve made to the coverage summary and treatments.

3

Confirm

Click Retry and Reset to proceed. The system re-runs the eligibility check. The patient status returns to In Draft once complete.

Retry limit
For a given service date, there is a maximum of 5 retries. Once reached, no further retries are possible for that appointment.

Handle a low-confidence VOB

Identify and resolve cases where the system couldn’t fully verify benefits.

2 min read
Version 2.3 · June 2026
1

Identify confidence from the dashboard

Confidence is shown by the coloured circle to the right of the patient name in the table.

ColourMeaning
GreenHigh confidence — benefits fully verified.
RedLow confidence, inactive insurance, or invalid patient details.
GreyUnsupported payer — the system couldn’t query this carrier.
2

Open the record

Click the patient row. The header banner shows the confidence score and turns red for low confidence or green for high confidence.

3

Read the VOB document

Open the Benefits Document. A “Low confidence Benefits” flag appears at the top. Scroll to the Comments section to find the specific failure reason.

What to do next

  • Attempt a retry — this re-queries the payer and may resolve the issue. See H4: Request a new VOB.
  • If retries don’t resolve it — edit the document manually with the correct information from a payer call or portal. See H6: Edit a VOB.
  • Grey circle (unsupported payer) — the system can’t query this carrier yet. A manual VOB will be required. Contact your billing team.

Manually correct or complete a benefits document when the payer response is missing data or the system flagged low confidence.

Open the Benefits Document

2

Edit fields as needed

Click any field to edit it. Common corrections include deductible amounts, OOP balances, copay values, and coinsurance percentages. Cross-reference against a payer portal or call notes.

Open the Cost Calculator

Detailed cost calculator (Staff view)

Full control over rates, coins/copay, service order, and what counts toward the grand total.

2 min read
Version 2.3 · June 2026
1

Switch to Staff Edit

From the Cost Calculator, click the Staff Edit tab at the top. The table expands with additional editable columns.

ColumnWhat it shows
CPT CodeProcedure code
DescriptionTreatment name, quantity, build type badge
Allowed/UnitContracted rate — editable
QtyUnits — editable
SubtotalAllowed × Qty
CoinsCoinsurance % — editable
CopayCopay amount — editable
DeductibleDeductible applied to this line
PT OwesCalculated patient responsibility

2

Edit rates, coins, and copay

Click any editable field (Allowed/Unit, Coins, Copay) to edit directly, the same way as adjusting quantity. Use this to correct contracted rates or fix coinsurance percentages returned incorrectly by the payer.

3

Reorder services (drag and drop)

Drag the handle on the left of any row to reorder treatments. The deductible is applied top to bottom — the first treatment exhausts deductible before it carries to the next. Order affects patient cost.

Ordering tip
Reorder to match the expected claims processing order from the payer. If immunotherapy is typically billed before office visits, put it first.
4

Include / exclude from grand total

Use the Include toggle on the right of each row to exclude a CPT from the grand total. Excluded codes appear as Bill Per Visit items in the summary — quoted separately.

5

Done

Click Done at the top right to save your edits and return to the Doctor View. The grand total updates to reflect your changes.

How patients land on the dashboard

How the system automatically populates the verification queue — and when manual adds are needed.

1 min read
Version 2.3 · June 2026

Automated — pre-scheduled appointments (primary path)

The system runs on a configured schedule and automatically generates VOBs for upcoming appointments. No staff action is required.

  • Runs are typically triggered 3 days prior to the appointment and again day of appointment to catch any late additions.
  • Only appointments matching the configured appointment types are included. Your tenant configuration defines which appointment types are eligible — or all appointments can be included.
  • The run schedule and appointment type filters are set per clinic. See A3: Tenant settings for what’s configurable.
  • Patients appear on the dashboard automatically in In Draft status once the VOB is complete — no manual trigger needed.

Manual add — exception path

Used for walk-ins, same-day appointments not covered by the scheduled run, or urgent cases. Staff initiate this directly from the dashboard Operations panel.

See H2: Start a benefits inquiry for the full how-to.

How retries work

What happens behind the scenes when a VOB fails and how the system tries to recover automatically.

2 min read
Version 2.3 · June 2026

Automatic system retries

When a VOB call fails due to a recoverable error, the system retries automatically before surfacing a failure to the dashboard.

AttemptDelay
Retry 1Immediate
Retry 21 minute
Retry 3+Exponential backoff (doubles each time)
Max window8 hours

Automatic retries apply to recoverable errors — timeouts, rate limits (429), and standard EDI error codes (42, 80).

Agentic retry layer

For cases where simple retry won’t help, the system can attempt alternate approaches:

  • Retry with alternate NPIs (if multiple providers are configured)
  • Retry with alternate payer routing or state mappings
  • Retry with normalized patient name formats

Agentic retries do not modify original patient data or permanently overwrite source demographics.

Manual retries (staff-initiated)

Staff can manually trigger a Re-VOB from within the patient record. This is a different action from automatic retries — it re-runs the full benefits inquiry process and will overwrite any manual edits made to the document.

Limit: 5 manual retries per service date. After this limit, no further retries are possible for that appointment date.

See H4: Request a new VOB for the full how-to.

How costs are calculated

How the system turns payer benefit data into patient responsibility figures.

2 min read
Version 2.3 · June 2026

Step 1 — Fee schedules (allowed amounts)

Every CPT code has a contracted allowed amount per payer — what the insurance carrier has agreed to pay for that service. These are stored in your clinic’s fee schedules and updated periodically.

Allowed amounts are the starting point for every cost calculation. If an allowed amount is incorrect for a payer, it can be edited in the Staff Edit view before confirming.

Step 2 — Cost share logic

Patient responsibility is calculated in this order for each CPT code:

  • Copay — if applicable, applied first regardless of deductible status. A copay is assessed whether or not deductible has been met.
  • Deductible — applied next, from top to bottom based on treatment order in the table. The deductible balance is exhausted progressively as you move down the service list. This is why treatment order matters in the Staff Edit view.
  • Coinsurance — once the deductible is met, the patient pays their coinsurance percentage of the remaining allowed amount.
  • Out-of-Pocket cap — once individual or family OOP is exhausted, the patient owes $0 for subsequent services.

Step 3 — Dynamic recalculation

Costs recalculate in real time as you add, remove, or reorder treatments. The Estimated Grand Total at the bottom of the calculator reflects the cumulative patient responsibility across all included services.

Per-visit costs (excluded from the total via the Include toggle) are shown separately below the grand total.

The cost estimate is based on current benefit data and fee schedules. Final costs may vary based on how the insurance carrier processes the actual claim — this is noted in the disclaimer on every printed report.

Appendix · A1

Insurance terminology

Plain-language definitions of the insurance terms you’ll encounter in benefits documents and cost calculations.

2 min read
Version 2.3 · June 2026
TermPlain-language definition
DeductibleThe amount a patient pays out of pocket before insurance starts covering costs. Resets each benefit year. Individual and family deductibles track separately.
Out-of-Pocket Maximum (OOP)The most a patient will pay in a year before insurance covers 100%. Once reached, the patient owes $0 for covered services. Includes deductible + coinsurance + copays (in most plans).
CoinsuranceThe percentage of costs the patient pays after the deductible is met. If coinsurance is 20%, the patient pays 20% and insurance pays 80%.
CopayA fixed dollar amount charged per visit or service, regardless of deductible status. A $40 office copay is owed whether or not the deductible is met.
Copay applies before deductibleWhen a plan has both a copay and a deductible for a service, the copay is charged first. The deductible balance is separate and irrelevant to whether the copay applies.
In-NetworkThe provider has a contract with the insurance carrier. Lower cost share for the patient.
Out-of-Network (OON)No contract with the carrier. Patient pays more, sometimes everything. OON benefits still display in the system if returned by the payer.
Prior Authorization (Prior Auth)Approval required from the insurance carrier before certain services are covered. If Prior Auth Required shows “Yes,” the clinic must obtain authorization before the visit.
Referral RequiredSome HMO plans require a referral from the patient’s PCP before seeing a specialist. If shown as “Yes,” confirm the referral is in place.
Benefit YearThe 12-month period over which deductible and OOP accumulators are tracked. Most plans follow a calendar year (Jan–Dec) but some use custom fiscal years. When the benefit year resets, deductible and OOP start over from zero.
Plan YearThe period covered by the insurance policy. Often aligns with benefit year. Shown as “Calendar” or a specific date range.
Split billingWhen services from the same visit are billed across multiple CPT codes, each potentially subject to different cost-share rules. The order of claims processing can affect how deductible is applied.
